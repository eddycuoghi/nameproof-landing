Why trademark screening is only the beginning

A name can be clear of trademarks and still be the wrong choice. Here is what a trademark search does not tell you.

×

SEO collision

Your name may be trademark-clear but completely buried in search results by stronger brands or generic terms. Discovery becomes very expensive from day one.

×

Social and domain conflicts

A trademark search does not check whether the .com or social handles are available. You can have a clear trademark and still be locked out of the name online.

×

Brand clarity and pronunciation

A name can be legally clear but hard to pronounce, confusing in other languages, or simply not memorable enough to build a long-term brand around.

UK founders: UKIPO is a separate check

Post-Brexit, the UK trademark system operates independently. A name clear in the EU may already be registered in the UK — and vice versa.

UKIPO operates independently from EUIPO

Since Brexit, new EU trademarks no longer automatically cover the UK. If you plan to operate in both markets, you need separate checks — and the NameProof Decision Framework covers both.

Companies House is not a trademark check

Registering a company name at Companies House prevents identical names but does not protect you from trademark claims. A UKIPO trademark review is a separate and necessary step.
